VAULTSCAN — LIVE DEMO SPEECH (English)
=====================================
~4–6 minutes depending on demo pace


1. OPENING
----------
Good morning. Today we will walk you through VaultScan — our Cloud Security
Posture Management platform. VaultScan finds dangerous cloud misconfigurations,
explains how attackers can chain them, helps fix them safely, and proves the
work with reports and email alerts. We will move through the product the same
way a real team would use it.


2. SETTINGS — CONNECTING THE CLOUD
----------------------------------
First, we open the Settings section. This is where the connection begins.
Here we enter our cloud credentials — carefully, with limited access — or we
switch to Demo mode when we want a safe practice environment. Once the
connection is saved, VaultScan is ready to scan without asking for keys on
every visit.

[On screen: Settings → Cloud Connection → Save → Test Connection]


3. SCHEDULED CHECKS & GMAIL ALERTS — CONTINUOUS PROTECTION
----------------------------------------------------------
One of the most important strengths of VaultScan is that security does not stop
when you leave the dashboard. In the same Settings area, you can configure a
timer and your Gmail. With that, VaultScan can run automatic scans at any
interval you choose — for example every hour, every 45 minutes, or every day.
When the scan finishes, a clear alert is sent by email from VaultScan Company.
That means you can see the result in your own inbox without signing into the
system again.

And when something serious appears, you are not left alone with a red list.
Our integrated LLM assistant helps you understand critical and other
misconfigurations in plain language, and guides you on how to stop the damage
quickly.

[On screen: enable schedule + Gmail alerts → enter your Gmail → Save]


4. LAUNCH SCAN — FROM CONNECTION TO EVIDENCE
--------------------------------------------
Because the connection is ready, we now run a live scan and look at the
results. Press Launch active scan. VaultScan inspects the cloud configuration,
scores the posture, and produces findings with evidence.

[On screen: Launch Active Scan]


5. OVERVIEW — RISK AT A GLANCE
------------------------------
In the Overview, you immediately see how exposed the service is — from critical
issues down to lower-severity misconfigurations. This is the command room for
managers and engineers at the same time: one health score, severity breakdown,
and clear status of the environment.

We also show that the process is aligned with industry standards — mapping
findings to well-known security and compliance expectations, not inventing
random checks. On the chart, you can watch how the security level changes after
each scan — improving when issues are fixed, or dropping when new risks appear.


6. GENERATE REPORT & FINDINGS — PROOF YOU CAN SHARE
---------------------------------------------------
Once a scan is complete, every problem and its remediation path can be opened
in detail. From Generate Report, you can create and download a professional
package — including explanations, priorities, and practical fix guidance —
ready for leadership or auditors.

In the activity stream and history views, you can filter and review problems
discovered over time. The same issues are fully documented in the Findings tab:
what is wrong, why it matters, how severe it is, and how to correct it.


7. ATTACK PATHS — UNDERSTANDING THE REAL DANGER
-----------------------------------------------
Now we move to Attack Paths. This section is critical for deep understanding.
VaultScan does not only say “there is a problem.” It shows an attack chain —
how small mistakes combine into a real breach story.

Here you can see how a single weak setting can grow into full information
exposure, and how the attack radius expands step by step. That is the
difference between a checklist and true risk storytelling.

[On screen: open a critical path — walk the chain]


8. FIXING OPTIONS — FROM DETECTION TO SAFE REPAIR
-------------------------------------------------
VaultScan does not stop at scanning. In Fixing Options, you can resolve issues
without fighting a confusing console full of raw cloud menus.

First, we choose Plan all fixes. The platform builds a map of every problem
that should be corrected. For each item, our model provides guidance and
context so the team knows what will change and why.

After reviewing the plan, you type the confirmation phrase and select Apply
fixes. Misconfigurations are then corrected at the source. While the process
runs, the system takes a snapshot of the previous state.

With Fix change report, you can compare in detail how the cloud was configured
before and what it looks like after the problems were resolved. And if anything
unexpected happens, you can reverse the changes with Please make it as before —
a controlled rollback path.

Every important action is also expressed as CLI commands. You can copy them and
run them directly in your cloud environment if you prefer a terminal workflow.

[On screen: Plan → Apply → Fix change report → optional rollback]


9. SCAN HISTORY — SECURITY AS A PROCESS
---------------------------------------
Finally, in Scan History, every scan operation is recorded. Security is not a
one-time event — it is continuous care. Here you can prove progress over time:
before and after fixes, and after each scheduled check.


10. CLOSING
-----------
To summarize: VaultScan connects carefully, scans continuously, explains risk
clearly, maps attack paths, fixes safely with plan–apply–rollback, emails
results to your inbox, and keeps a full history of the journey. That is how we
turn cloud misconfigurations from silent open doors into a controlled,
auditable security process.

Scan the vault. Secure the cloud. Before someone else does.
Thank you — we are ready for questions.
